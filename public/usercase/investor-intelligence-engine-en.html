The Problem

INFORMATION OVERLOAD,
INSIGHT DROUGHT

The modern investor has more data available than ever. But more data without better filtering creates analysis paralysis, not alpha.

#
Pain Point
What It Costs You
01
RSS & News Overload
You subscribe to 30+ newsletters, 15 RSS feeds, and 3 financial data services. Reading everything takes 3 hours. Missing the one signal that matters happens constantly.
02
Manual Earnings Tracking
Earnings season means juggling 40 companies across 3 weeks. You can't track every call, every guidance revision, every analyst upgrade. One miss and your position is wrong for a month.
03
Deal Flow Blind Spots
Great early-stage deals die in your inbox or never reach you at all. Finding startups that match your thesis across YC batches, AngelList, and founder networks is a part-time job.
04
Portfolio Drift
A great run in tech means your allocation drifts from 40% to 68% without you noticing. Manual portfolio review happens monthly at best — but markets move daily.
05
Research Report Overwhelm
Analyst reports, 10-Ks, S-1s, and competitor teardowns pile up. A single S-1 is 200 pages. You're either reading too little or reading everything and making slow decisions.
06
No Systematic Second Opinion
You catch your own blind spots only in hindsight. There's no consistent framework stress-testing your thesis — just your gut and whatever you read this morning.

ThesisDebater — System Prompt 
# ThesisDebater Instructions

You are a rigorous, skeptical investment
analyst. When given an investment thesis,
your job is to CHALLENGE it, not validate.

ALWAYS PRODUCE:
1. Top 3 bear case arguments
   (be specific, not generic)
2. 2-3 comparable failures
   with similar thesis/market
3. Key assumptions investor
   might be rationalizing
4. Questions to ask the founder
5. Kill conditions — what would
   make you exit immediately?

DO NOT: validate or encourage.
Your job is to find flaws.

Tone: direct, analytical, skeptical.
No softening language.
