Pain Points

Patient Overload,
Clinician Burnout

Managing 800+ patients with chronic disease tracking, literature updates, and administrative tasks pushes every family physician past capacity.

#
Pain Point
Cost
01
Patient Follow-Up Falls Behind
800+ patients with chronic conditions need regular tracking. Manually tracking follow-up dates, lab results, and medication changes is nearly impossible. Missed follow-ups = worsening outcomes.
02
Medical Literature Explosion
Thousands of new papers published weekly across diabetes, hypertension, and cardiovascular domains. Manual screening can't achieve comprehensive coverage. Missing a critical guideline update directly impacts care quality.
03
Administrative Paperwork Burden
Chart notes, referral letters, insurance claims — 2+ hours daily on non-clinical work. Every minute spent on paperwork is stolen from patient care.
04
Fragmented Chronic Care
No systematic population health tracking. Can't quickly identify which patients have worsening HbA1c, who needs medication changes, or who has fallen out of follow-up.
05
Repetitive Patient Education
Explaining the same disease knowledge, medication instructions, and lifestyle advice dozens of times daily. High-frequency repetition drains energy, and verbal communication has low information retention.
06
Clinic Operations Inefficiency
Scheduling conflicts, high no-show rates, long wait times. Without data-driven scheduling optimization, clinic capacity utilization stays below 70%.

ChartAssist — System Prompt 
# ChartAssist Instructions

You are a professional medical documentation
assistant. Based on consultation conversation
records, generate standardized clinical docs.

Always produce:
1. SOAP-format clinical notes
   (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan)

2. Structured medication list
   with dosage, frequency, precautions
3. Referral letters (if needed)
   with complete history summary
4. Insurance claim materials
   with ICD-10 codes
5. Follow-up plan
   with next examination dates

Do NOT: Make clinical diagnoses or
prescribing decisions.
Your job is documentation, not medicine.

Tone: Professional, concise, standardized.
Comply with medical documentation standards.
