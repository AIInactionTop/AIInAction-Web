Pain Points

Talent Buried,
Deals Won By Luck

Freelance designers have world-class skills but spend half their time hunting clients, negotiating prices, and drowning in admin work.

#
Pain Point
Cost
01
Finding Clients Is a Needle in a Haystack
8-10 hours per week scrolling Upwork, Dribbble, LinkedIn — writing proposals, responding to inquiries. One in ten leads is actually worth pursuing. The rest are lowball bids or ghosts.
02
Pricing Paralysis
Price too low and you lose money. Price too high and you lose the client. No market benchmark data — every quote is a gut feeling. Took a $3K brand project, then learned the market rate was $8K. Half the work for free.
03
The Endless Revision Spiral
"One more tweak" becomes an infinite loop. No clear revision limits, no status tracking. Three rounds become five, five become eight — every extra revision eats into profit.
04
Admin Devours Creative Time
Contracts, invoices, payment chasing, taxes, file management, email replies — these eat 10+ hours every week. Actual design time is less than half of your working hours.
05
Portfolio Always Outdated
Finish a project, rush to the next one. Behance and your personal site haven't been updated in six months. Potential clients see last year's work. Best case studies are forever "waiting for free time."
06
Cash Flow Roller Coaster
$15K this month, $3K the next. No pipeline visibility, no idea what revenue looks like three months out. Quarterly taxes are a fog — deductible expenses routinely missed.

# ProjectPilot Instructions

You are a professional design project
manager. Your job is to ensure every
project is delivered on time, on budget,
while protecting the designer's
creative time.

Core Responsibilities:
1. Create project timelines & milestones
   (based on scope and complexity)

2. Track revision counts
   Auto-alert when exceeding agreed rounds
3. Send daily progress updates to clients
   Reduce "how's it going?" messages
4. Remind 48h before deadlines
5. Flag risks — what could cause
   delivery delays?

Do NOT: Make design decisions for
the designer. Your job is process.

Tone: Professional, friendly, efficient.
Gentle with clients, strict with time.
