The Problem

PMs Drown in Process,
Not Product

The best PMs spend less than 20% of their time on actual product thinking. The rest is documentation, meetings, and information hunting.

01
The PRD Never-Ending Draft
Writing a solid PRD takes 8–16 hours. Updating it after each stakeholder review adds another 4 hours. Multiply by 6 features per quarter.
02
Competitor Intelligence Is Stale
By the time you compile a competitor analysis, it's already outdated. Manually tracking 10+ competitors across product, pricing, and marketing is untenable.
03
Meeting Insights Disappear
User interviews, design reviews, sprint retrospectives — critical insights buried in 50-page transcripts nobody reads. Decisions get repeated, contexts get lost.
04
User Feedback Is Scattered
Feedback lives in Intercom, Zendesk, Slack, app store reviews, and NPS surveys. No unified signal. You can't see patterns without spending a full day aggregating manually.
05
Alignment Is Endless
Engineering needs specs. Design needs user context. Sales needs a product brief. Marketing needs positioning. Every team needs a different version of the same story.
06
Strategy Work Gets Zero Time
After all the above, there's no time left to think about product direction, market trends, or 12-month roadmap. Fire-fighting replaces strategy.
The Solution

Your 4-Agent
PM Ops Network

Each agent owns a critical PM workflow. Together they give you back 20+ hours a week — and dramatically better outputs.

🔍
MarketRadar
Competitive Intelligence Agent
Monitors 15 competitors daily — tracking product updates, pricing changes, job postings (a signal for strategic direction), press releases, and app store reviews. Delivers a weekly competitive digest with strategic implications, not just raw data.
web_search web_scrape summarize trend_detect email_send
Competitor monitoring time/week 8h → 0
📋
DocuBot
Requirements Documentation Agent
Takes your rough feature brief (even a voice note or Slack thread) and generates a structured PRD with problem statement, user stories, acceptance criteria, and edge cases. Formats for Notion, Confluence, or Linear. Updates automatically when you provide feedback.
prd_template user_stories notion_write feedback_edit
PRD creation time 12h → 2h
🎙️
MeetingMind
Meeting Intelligence Agent
Processes meeting transcripts and recordings. Extracts action items, key decisions, open questions, and product insights. Tags insights by theme and links them to relevant features or OKRs. Generates follow-up emails and updates Jira tickets automatically.
transcript_parse action_extract jira_update email_draft
Post-meeting admin time 45min → 5min
📣
FeedbackPulse
User Feedback Aggregation Agent
Pulls feedback from Intercom, Zendesk, app store reviews, NPS survey responses, and your Slack customer channel. Clusters by theme, quantifies frequency, and links to product areas. Delivers a weekly signal report that tells you what your users are actually saying — not what you think they're saying.
intercom_pull app_store_scrape cluster_analysis theme_tag report_gen

DocuBot — System Prompt (excerpt) 
# DocuBot — PRD Generation Agent

You generate structured Product Requirements
Documents from rough input (Slack threads,
voice transcripts, bullet points).

OUTPUT STRUCTURE:
1. Problem Statement (2-3 sentences)
2. User Stories (Given/When/Then format)
3. Acceptance Criteria (numbered list)
4. Edge Cases & Out of Scope
5. Open Questions for PM review
6. Success Metrics (leading + lagging)

Format for Notion API.
Add [ARIA REVIEW NEEDED] tags on sections
where you lack sufficient context.
